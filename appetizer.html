Lumpia

I. Preparing the Lumpia Filling
The heart of lumpia lies in its flavorful filling, which typically consists of ground pork, vegetables, and seasonings. Here's how to prepare it:

1.Gather your ingredients: You'll need ground pork, minced onions, minced carrots, chopped parsley, garlic powder, ground black pepper, salt, sesame oil, and eggs. Some recipes also include other vegetables like cabbage or green onions.

2.Cook the meat: In a wok or large skillet, cook the ground pork over medium-high heat until it's crumbly and browned. Drain off excess grease, leaving a thin coating in the pan.

3.Sauté aromatics: Add minced onions and garlic to the pan and cook until fragrant. This step builds a flavorful base for the filling.

4.Combine ingredients: Return the cooked pork to the pan. Add the minced carrots, parsley, and any other vegetables you're using. Season with garlic powder, ground black pepper, salt, and sesame oil. Stir well to combine.

5.Bind the mixture: Crack the eggs into the filling mixture and stir thoroughly. The eggs act as a binder, helping the filling hold its shape during frying.

6.Cool the filling: Remove the filling from the heat and let it cool completely before proceeding to the next step. This prevents the wrappers from becoming soggy and makes handling easier.

II. Wrapping the Lumpia

Now that your filling is ready, it's time to assemble the lumpia:

1.Prepare your wrappers: Lumpia wrappers are thin, almost translucent sheets made from flour, water, and sometimes eggs. You can find them in Asian grocery stores.

2.Position the wrapper: Lay a wrapper flat on a plate, orienting it like a diamond with one corner pointing upwards.

3.Add the filling: Place a spoonful of the cooled filling diagonally near one corner of the wrapper, leaving space at both ends.

4.Roll and tuck: Fold the bottom corner of the wrapper over the filling. Fold in the sides and then roll tightly towards the top corner, creating a compact cylinder.

5.Seal the edge: Moisten the top corner of the wrapper with a little water or beaten egg to seal the lumpia.

6.Repeat: Continue assembling the remaining lumpia until all the filling and wrappers are used.

III. Frying and Serving

The final step is frying the lumpia to golden perfection:

1.Heat the oil: Heat a generous amount of oil in a deep pot or wok over medium heat. The oil should be hot enough that a small piece of wrapper sizzles when dropped in.

2.Fry in batches: Carefully place the lumpia in the hot oil, frying in batches to avoid overcrowding. Overcrowding can lower the oil temperature and result in soggy lumpia.

3.Cook until golden: Fry the lumpia, turning occasionally, until they are golden brown and crispy. This usually takes several minutes per side.

4.Drain and serve: Remove the cooked lumpia from the oil and place them on a wire rack or paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

5.Dipping sauces: Serve hot lumpia with your favorite dipping sauce. Popular choices include sweet and sour sauce, banana ketchup, or a vinegar-based dipping sauce.



Dynamine

1.Prepare the filling: In a pan, cook the ground pork until browned. Add the garlic, onion, and carrots, and cook until softened. Season with black pepper, salt, and ginisa mix. Stir in the egg and breadcrumbs.

2.Stuff the chilies: Slit the green chilies lengthwise and carefully remove the seeds and membranes. Fill each chili with the pork mixture and a piece of cheddar cheese.

3.Wrap and fry: Wrap each stuffed chili in a spring roll wrapper, sealing the edges with water or beaten egg. Deep fry in hot oil until golden brown and crispy.

4.Serve: Drain the fried dynamite on paper towels. Serve hot with a dipping sauce of mayonnaise and garlic.



Cheesesticks

1.Freeze the Cheese: Freeze the string cheese completely before breading. This crucial step prevents the cheese from melting too quickly during frying.

2.Prepare the Breading Station: Set up three shallow bowls: one with flour mixed with seasonings, one with beaten eggs, and one with a mixture of breadcrumbs and panko.

3.Bread the Cheese: Dredge each frozen cheese stick in the flour, then the egg, then back in the flour, and finally coat thoroughly with the breadcrumb mixture. This double-dipping technique ensures a thick, crispy crust.

4.Freeze Again: Freeze the breaded cheese sticks for at least 30 minutes to solidify the breading and further prevent cheese leakage.

5.Fry: Heat the oil to 350-375°F (175-190°C). Fry the cheese sticks in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 1-2 minutes.

6.Drain and Serve: Drain the fried cheese sticks on paper towels. Serve immediately with your favorite dipping sauce, such as marinara sauce or ranch dressing.
